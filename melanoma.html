Melanoma

Melanoma is a type of skin cancer that develops from the pigment-producing cells known as melanocytes. It is the most serious type of skin cancer due to its ability to spread to other parts of the body.

Symptoms

  • A change in an existing mole
  • The development of a new, unusual growth on the skin
  • Moles that are asymmetrical, have irregular borders, or vary in color

Causes

Melanoma is primarily caused by exposure to ultraviolet (UV) radiation from the sun or tanning beds. Other factors include:

  • Genetics
  • Having a large number of moles or atypical moles
  • Weakened immune system

Seriousness

Melanoma can be life-threatening if not detected early. Regular skin checks and monitoring for changes in moles can help in early detection and treatment.
